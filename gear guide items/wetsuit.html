Wetsuit

Purpose

A wetsuit is an essential piece of gear for any surfer who wants to extend their time in the water, especially in colder conditions. By trapping a thin layer of water between the suit and your skin, it provides thermal insulation, keeping you warm even when the water is chilly. Whether you’re surfing in cool or frigid waters, a wetsuit helps maintain your body temperature, allowing you to stay comfortable and focused on the waves without the distraction of the cold. Wetsuits also provide protection from the sun, stings, and scrapes, adding to their functionality.

What to Look For

When choosing a wetsuit, it’s important to consider factors such as fit, material, and thickness. A well-fitting wetsuit ensures that you stay warm and have full mobility while paddling and riding waves. Look for wetsuits made from high-quality neoprene, as this material is flexible and durable. The thickness of the wetsuit will depend on the water temperature; thinner wetsuits are better for warmer climates, while thicker ones are needed for colder conditions. Be sure to check for reinforced seams and a smooth, snug seal at the wrists and ankles to prevent water from entering and to maximize warmth.
