Leash

Purpose

The surfboard leash is a vital safety tool that keeps your board attached to you after a wipeout. It prevents the board from drifting away, protecting both you and other surfers around you. Whether you're a beginner or a seasoned rider, using a leash ensures you can quickly recover your board and get back to catching waves without wasting energy paddling after it.

What to Look For

When shopping for a leash, consider its length, thickness, and cuff comfort. A leash should be roughly the same length as your board, with thicker cords offering better durability in bigger waves. Look for strong swivels to prevent tangling and padded ankle cuffs with secure Velcro closures for a snug but comfortable fit. A high-quality leash balances strength and flexibility, giving you peace of mind in the surf.
